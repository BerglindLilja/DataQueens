}}'''Borlas''' was a [[Men of Gondor|Man]] of [[Gondor]] in the [[Fourth Age]], and a key figure in [[Tolkien|J.R.R. Tolkien]]'s short-lived story ''[[The New Shadow]]''. He had two children, an unnamed daughter and a son [[Berelach]], a friend of [[Saelon]].<ref name="shadow" />

== Biography ==

Borlas was the son of [[Beregond (Captain)|Beregond]], the captain who saved [[Faramir]] from being burned by [[Denethor II|Denethor]]. After the [[War of the Ring]], he lived in [[Pen-arduin]], which was in [[Emyn Arnen]].<ref>''[[The History of Middle-earth]]'', Vol. XII: ''[[The Peoples of Middle-earth]]'', Index, pg. 471</ref> Sometime in the past he caught a [[Saelon|young man]] stealing an apple from his garden. He scolded him describing his behavior as "[[Orc]] work" but took the boy under his wing becoming his master. Years later, he learned through him of the growing shadow among [[Men]] and the possibility of a [[The New Shadow Cult|secret cult]] worshiping the old enemies of the world.<ref name="shadow">''[[The History of Middle-earth]]'', Vol. XII: ''[[The Peoples of Middle-earth]]'', chapter XVI: "[[The New Shadow]]"</ref>

== Etymology ==

''Borlas'' is Sindarin for "Enduring joy" from ''bor'' ("endure") and ''glass'' or ''galas'' ("joy").<ref>''The History of Middle-earth'', Vol. V: ''[[The Lost Road and Other Writings]]'', Part Three: "[[The Etymologies]]"</ref>
